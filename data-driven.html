Data Driven

Published: Sun 05 June 2016
By Ray

For quite some time now it has been "trendy" for individuals, teams and organization to say they are data driven. Even if it the effectiveness is not there, it is a wonderful aspiration. I'm 100% on-board with using data as a tool to drive decisions. Data have always been relevant to decision making, however, in recent years, everything data, are the rock stars of the software industry.

Having completed a number of data science and machine learning MOOCs, I have come to the conclusion that a foundation in data science improves one's ability to be an effective data driven practitioner.

What is data-driven? Probably different things to different people. However, for this discussion, this will suffice: "The adjective data-driven means that progress in an activity is compelled by data, rather than by intuition or personal experience. It is often labeled as business jargon for what scientists call evidence-based decision making." Link

What is data science? This definition works for me. "Data science is an interdisciplinary field about processes and systems to extract knowledge or insights from data in various forms, either structured or unstructured, which is a continuation of some of the data analysis fields such as statistics, data mining, and predictive analytics" Link

What is a data scientist? Apparently, it is a synonym for unicorn. Link

What are some benefits of having a foundation in the principles of data sciences?

  1. The skills and ability to root out vanity metrics
  2. The skills and ability to recognize being fooled by randomness or bias
  3. The skills to manage the bias-variance trade-off

As more and more of our daily life become driven by machine learning algorithms in one form or another, let's not forget this insightful and important maxim:

"all models are wrong, but some are useful" George Box
